# Community Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a respectful, harassment-free community for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics,
gender identity or expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic
status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

## Expected behavior

Community members must:

- Be respectful, constructive, and considerate.
- Critique ideas and code rather than people.
- Accept responsibility, apologize for mistakes, and learn from feedback.
- Respect privacy, confidentiality, and differing viewpoints.
- Prioritize the safety and health of the community.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized
language or imagery, trolling, sustained disruption, deliberate intimidation,
doxing, and publishing another person's private information without permission.

## Scope

This policy applies in project repositories, issue trackers, reviews,
discussions, events, and other spaces where someone represents the project.

## Reporting

For immediate threats or illegal conduct, contact local emergency services or
the relevant platform. GitHub-hosted abuse can be reported privately through
[GitHub Support](https://support.github.com/contact/report-abuse).

For project moderation, ask a maintainer for a private contact channel without
including sensitive details in a public issue. Reports are handled
confidentially to the extent possible. Retaliation against a reporter or
participant in an investigation is prohibited.

## Enforcement

Maintainers will review reports promptly, avoid conflicts of interest, gather
only necessary information, and communicate an outcome when appropriate.
Consequences are proportional to impact, intent, and history:

1. A private correction and explanation.
2. A formal warning with conditions for continued participation.
3. Temporary exclusion from project interaction.
4. Permanent exclusion for severe or repeated violations.

Maintainers who violate this policy are subject to the same process. Decisions
may be reconsidered when material new information becomes available.
